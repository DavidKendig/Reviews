Movies

Films I've watched, rated, and reviewed.

A View to a Kill

1985 · Roger Moore

★★★☆☆

Moore's final Bond outing. Christopher Walken is a memorable villain, but the film shows its age.

Casino Royale

2006 · Daniel Craig

★★★★★

A masterful reboot. Craig brings raw intensity to Bond, and the poker scenes are riveting.

Diamonds Are Forever

1971 · Sean Connery

★★★☆☆

Connery's return is welcome but the campy tone and Las Vegas setting make it uneven.

Die Another Day

2002 · Pierce Brosnan

★★☆☆☆

Over-the-top CGI and an invisible car pushed this one too far into sci-fi territory.

Dr. No

1962 · Sean Connery

★★★★☆

The one that started it all. Connery defines the role with effortless cool.

For Your Eyes Only

1981 · Roger Moore

★★★★☆

A grounded, back-to-basics Bond film. One of Moore's strongest entries in the series.

From Russia with Love

1963 · Sean Connery

★★★★★

A taut Cold War thriller. The train fight with Red Grant is one of the best action sequences in the franchise.

GoldenEye

1995 · Pierce Brosnan

★★★★★

Brosnan's debut is electric. The tank chase through St. Petersburg is iconic, and Sean Bean delivers as 006.

Goldfinger

1964 · Sean Connery

★★★★★

The quintessential Bond film. Oddjob, the Aston Martin DB5, and "No, Mr. Bond, I expect you to die."

Licence to Kill

1989 · Timothy Dalton

★★★★☆

Dalton goes rogue and it's gripping. Darker than most Bond films of its era and ahead of its time.

Live and Let Die

1973 · Roger Moore

★★★☆☆

Moore's debut brings a lighter tone. The boat chase is fun but the voodoo elements haven't aged well.

Moonraker

1979 · Roger Moore

★★★☆☆

Bond in space. Pure spectacle that traded espionage for sci-fi, but Jaws' return is welcome.

No Time to Die

2021 · Daniel Craig

★★★★☆

A bold and emotional send-off for Craig's Bond. The ending is divisive but undeniably powerful.

Octopussy

1983 · Roger Moore

★★★☆☆

A fun Cold War adventure with a memorable circus sequence, though the clown disguise is divisive.

On Her Majesty's Secret Service

1969 · George Lazenby

★★★★☆

Underrated gem. Lazenby's only outing has genuine emotion and one of the best endings in the series.

Quantum of Solace

2008 · Daniel Craig

★★★☆☆

A direct sequel to Casino Royale that's relentless but lacks the depth of its predecessor.

Skyfall

2012 · Daniel Craig

★★★★★

Visually stunning with Deakins' cinematography. Bardem is a terrifying villain. Bond at its modern best.

Spectre

2015 · Daniel Craig

★★★☆☆

The opening tracking shot in Mexico City is phenomenal, but the Blofeld twist falls flat.

The Living Daylights

1987 · Timothy Dalton

★★★★☆

Dalton brings a serious, grounded edge. A refreshing change after Moore's lighter era.

The Man with the Golden Gun

1974 · Roger Moore

★★★☆☆

Christopher Lee is a worthy adversary, but the film can't decide between serious and silly.

The Spy Who Loved Me

1977 · Roger Moore

★★★★☆

Moore's finest hour. The Lotus submarine, Jaws, and the ski jump opening are all iconic.

The World Is Not Enough

1999 · Pierce Brosnan

★★★☆☆

Sophie Marceau shines as Elektra King, but Denise Richards as a nuclear physicist is hard to buy.

Thunderball

1965 · Sean Connery

★★★★☆

Gorgeous underwater sequences and peak Connery swagger. A bit slow in spots but still classic Bond.

Tomorrow Never Dies

1997 · Pierce Brosnan

★★★★☆

A prescient media mogul villain and great chemistry with Michelle Yeoh. Underrated Brosnan entry.

You Only Live Twice

1967 · Sean Connery

★★★☆☆

The volcano lair is spectacular but the disguise plotline is problematic. Still, it's grand-scale Bond.
